Data visualizations to guide an equitable energy transition

PEOPLE

People use energy to prepare food, keep warm or cool, illuminate their homes, entertain themselves, and move from one place to another. Lack of access to affordable clean energy is a major source of inequity.

PLANET

The extraction, processing, and use of energy causes environmental degradation from local to global scales. Climate change is already causing immense environmental and social harm that is disproportionately felt by socially vulnerable populations.

ENERGY

Changes in energy sources and conversion technologies have profound impacts on the well-being of people and the planet. The current energy transition must be steered in a direction that improves human well-being and the health of natural systems.
